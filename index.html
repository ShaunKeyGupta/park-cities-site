The Difference

One dealer gives you one number.

A dealer's offer is a single buyer's price, and that buyer is paid to give you the least the market will forgive. An auction places a collection in front of the collectors who have waited years for it, and lets them compete.

Our office exists for families and estates holding significant collections: to document what is actually there, establish what it is actually worth in writing, and, when the family chooses, place it at auction with every detail handled.

The Process

Handled once. Handled properly.

i.

Private consultation

In our Highland Park office or at your home. A complete walkthrough of the collection, at no cost and with no obligation.

ii.

Written valuation

A documented valuation of the collection, delivered within five business days, suitable for the estate file.

iii.

Insured intake

Door-to-door insured transport, photographed intake, and a chain-of-custody record for every item from the moment it leaves your hands.

iv.

Grading and catalog

Significant pieces are professionally graded and encapsulated, then photographed and placed in the appropriate auction catalog.

v.

One settlement

A single settlement wire, 30 to 45 days after buyer payment is received, with a full accounting of every lot. One dedicated point of contact throughout.
